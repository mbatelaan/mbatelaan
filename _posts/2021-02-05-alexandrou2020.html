Alexandrou2020

Nucleon axial and pseudoscalar form factors from lattice QCD at the physical point

  • partially conserved axial current (PCAC)
  • Goldberger-Treiman relation
  • Pion Pole Dominance (PPD)
  • Lattice calculations of Nucleon Form Factors
  • They use the method from Jang2019a where they determine the first excited state from the temporal component of the axial vector current, which is apparently very precise.

SUMMARY

  • The paper has a very nice structure, it is a good example of a clear 'story-telling' structure where it is easy to follow and includes all the important details.
  • The paper calculates the nucleon axial & pseudoscalar form factors at approximately the physical values and with high stats.
  • They apply the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) to their results, but for low momenta (<0.3 GeV2) the results from the direct extraction of the pseudoscalar matrix elements deviate from those calculated throught the PCAC. Further research is required here.
  • Pion Pole Dominance (PPD) is used when relating the pion-nucleon coupling to the form factors
  • Goldberger-Treiman relation (discrepancy) is mentioned as well
  • They include a nice description of the Q2-dependence of the form factors and how this can be modelled using a dipole ansatz or a model independent z-expansion
  • The lattice methodology chapter has a very good description of the two-point functions and the effect of overlap with excited states. Also includes details on the Gaussian smearing which most papers omit.

  • They use three methods to check for ground state dominance in the final result:

    1. the plateau method
    2. summation method
    3. two-exponential fit method
      1. Assume the first excited state is the same in the 2pt. and 3pt. correlators. Fit to the 2pt. function first and use the excited state parameters from this fit in the fit to the ratio
      2. Allow the excited state to be different between the 2pt. and the 3pt. correlators. The excited state of the 3pt. corrrelator is also a fit parameter.

    The plots of their results of all three methods are really clear and nice to look at.

  • The three point functions are calculated with a fixed sink time, and they choose seven soure-sink separations in the range 0.64fm to 1.60fm
  • Includes a detailed section on the renormalization functions
  • The results show that the 2-exponential fit method produces the best results but that the two ways of determining the excited state energy result in form factor values which do not agree, the paper claims that this is expected and seen in other papers as well.
  • The results show that at small Q2 (<0.4), the PCAC is not fulfilled. Further investigation is required here. They think it could be explained by the difference in excited state energies betweem the 2pt. and 3pt. functions.
