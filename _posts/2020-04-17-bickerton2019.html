1 2) Baryon matrix elements and generalized currents

The generalised currents are defined with flavour matrices in them. The dirac matrices used can be any corresponding to the vector, axial, scalar, pseudoscalar or tensor current. The matrix elements which will be computed are between Baryon states from the Baryon octet. For semi-leptonic decays, the standard model only allows for flavour diagonal neutral currents, so there are no flavour-changing neutral currents (FCNC). Stated that the goal of the article is to establish ways in which the form factors from the decomposition of the matrix elements depend on the transition taking place and on the quark masses. The main focus for flavour structures is that of bilinear operators, so the bilinear quark currents are expressed by the corresponding meson name.

1.1 Classifying form factors

Jacob defines a generalised current to be the folowing:

\begin{equation} \label{eq:1} J^{F(\mathcal{M})} = \bar{q}F\gamma^{(\mathcal{M})} q \end{equation}

He then shows that the hermitian conjugate of this current is equal to the current but with the flavour matrix F transposed:

\begin{equation} \label{eq:2} J^{F(\mathcal{M})\dagger} = \bar{q}F^{T}\gamma^{(\mathcal{M})} q \end{equation}

If a matrix elements between two baryons is then defined as

\begin{equation} \label{eq:3} A_{\bar{B}'FB} \equiv \bra{B', p', s'} J^{F(\mathcal{M})} (q) \ket{B, p, s} \end{equation}

From this we can then get the following relation:

\begin{equation} \label{eq:4} (A_{\bar{B}F^{T}B'})^{*} = A_{\bar{B}'FB} \end{equation}

This relation is interesting because it allows us to classify the form factors of matrix elements. Jacob applies this to the electromagnetic current where I should note that the transpose of F refers to flavour space, in the example in the paper, F is equal to \(q^{\mu(\mathcal{M})}\) (the momentum transfer) for the vector current and taking the transpose of this in flavour space gives a negative sign which tells us that the third form factor of the vector current will also have to get a negative sign when the complex conjugate is taken of the matrix element. The first two form factors will be unaltered because the flavour matrix F is unaltered when applying the relation above. Therefore, F1 and F2 are called first class form factors and F3 is second class form factor. important: This also means that when B=B', the second class form factors will vanish, so they are irrelevant for most of my work.
